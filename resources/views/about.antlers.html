Everything is
better when donuts
are involved

Job interviews are painful. But with a pocketful of donuts they are never a waste of time. Hate waiting at the DMV? Try pounding a dozen donuts and splattering jelly all over form 90210. You're welcome.

15. April 2025

Sure they're not healthy for you, but neither is the stress you carry when you spend time doing things you hate. All of these things are better with donuts.

Sugar Diplomacy

Donuts have a way of resolving conflict that no formal process ever could. Passive-aggressive email from accounting? Show up with a pink box and suddenly everyone is “circling back” with a much better attitude. Neighbors fighting over a hedge line? Glazed. Peace restored. World leaders spend billions on strategy, defense, and negotiation, yet somehow no one has seriously explored the stabilizing influence of a warm cruller. History may someday look back on this oversight with deep embarrassment.

Emotional Cushioning

Life insists on happening to you at full speed. Missed train. Weird text. Dentist appointment. The donut does not remove these burdens, but it does place a soft, frosted barrier between you and total psychological collapse. Experts agree that holding a donut in one hand makes any inconvenience feel 14% more manageable. This number was not verified, but it feels spiritually correct.

Tactical Celebration

Donuts are uniquely qualified for events that do not deserve a full cake but still require recognition. Survived Monday? Donut. Sent one difficult email? Donut. Made it through a social gathering without once saying “you too” to the waiter? Huge donut moment. Unlike other desserts, donuts understand the importance of immediacy. They ask for no plates, no ceremony, no long speech. They simply arrive, do the work, and leave a faint dusting of sugar as proof that joy briefly existed.
